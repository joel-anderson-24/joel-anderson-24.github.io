My Learning Plan

1. What is your long term goal or career pathway?

The ultimate goal is to make money doing something I enjoy. I'm a gamer through and through so my plan is to further develop my skills so that I might get into game development one day.

2. A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning.

My strengths would be my ability to go through the work thoroughly and properly the first time around so I don't usually need to revisit/don't usually miss anything out (though it does happen sometimes.) Another strength would be the fact that I enjoy what we've been doing so far and I find that leaves me wanting to do more. A weakness is definitely my typing speed. I broke my arm a few years back and the doctors didn't put the bone back in its correct place, leaving me unable to pronate so I've had to learn how to type all over again in a more ergonomic position.

3. What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to see how I develop as we move along into more teamwork-type roles. I used to be quite reserved, so I would like to learn how to communicate better to a team.

4. A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

I like to think it's a nice little community that we are learning in, so I know it's easy to reach out for help when and if needed. My usual style would be to break the problem up into bite-sized pieces and gnaw away at them one-by-one so it isn't as overwhelming.

5. A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way.

I would probably message the discord chats or reach out to a Facilitator as my first point of call, besides possibly asking my home group or cohort. Most likely on discord as this is the channel everyone is available on.

6. A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team.

Just always ready and willing to help and replying in a timely manner, which they already do. Probably sometimes dumbing it down for us newbies though haha.

7. Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things.

Nothing in particular besides a bi-monthly trip to Auckland to see my family, but this can be done on weekends.
