Identity & Values

1. Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

Since leaving home and moving in with my flatmates, I've become a lot more confident and accepted so it makes me more sure of myself. It allows me to be unashamedly me.

2. Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

My main strength would be patience. I think this will be useful for my learning journey because I won't feel uncomfortable if I were to take a while on a certain area, I wouldn't feel rushed or pressured either because I'm comfortable taking my time and being patient to make sure things are done properly.

I'm also meticulous and I like to complete things step-by-step and properly, so I think it'll help me to not miss any study material and to complete everything in the intended order without skipping ahead.

3. Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

I think my main limitation would be my typing speed. This is because I broke my arm a few years ago and struggle to pronate. So, I'm re-learning how to type and this has led to a slower typing speed than most I'm sure. It will simply slow me down, but won't stop me from completing what I'm supposed to.

4. A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

I like to think it's a nice little community that we are learning in, so I know it's easy to reach out for help when and if needed. My usual style would be to break the problem up into bite-sized pieces and gnaw away at them one-by-one so it isn't as overwhelming.

5. Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

I used to work as a picker packer and my main function was to either pick and pack orders or organise the stock we had. This was the same for many other team members, but a lot of the time the team members got lazy and wouldn't put things back in the right place. It took a few mix-ups for it to be sorted, but I brought it to my manager's attention without naming names. It was quite effective, however another approach I tried was talking to some individual team members and found that the main issue was that they genuinely didn't know where some parts went but didn't feel comfortable asking. It ended up that labels were put on the shelves to make it easier for everyone. I think if I were to do it again, I'd skip talking to the manager.
